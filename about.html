TEND.TO
TEND.TO is a digital website project designed and coded by Julien Ortiz as part of the 2023 Design Honours course at the University of Technology, Sydney. This project aims to use the online practice of digital gardening in order to offer a growing space to learn about community creative practice in Western Sydney and the Blue Mountains and plant seeds of inspiration to support the creation of diverse and collective futures.

I acknowledge the Dharug and Gadigal people of the Eora nation, whose lands I live and work on. I pay my respects to Elders past and present, recognising them as the traditional custodians of land, sea and sky, and that their sovereignty was never ceded. This was and always will be, Aboriginal land.
What is a digital garden?

“A garden is a collection of evolving ideas that aren't strictly organised by their publication date. They're inherently exploratory – notes are linked through contextual associations. They aren't refined or complete — notes are published as half-finished thoughts that will grow and evolve over time. They're less rigid, less performative, and less perfect than the personal websites we're used to seeing.”

— Maggie Appleton (2020)

TEND.TO draws from this framework by compiling informational resources that amplify and support the creative practices and initiatives of diverse, intersectional and BIPOC creatives within Western Sydney and the Blue Mountains Area. This space draws from my ongoing research and insights that have been initiated over the course of the 2023 year. It is a growing space, thoughts are added as I learn and develop as a budding creative and member of my local Western Sydney community. Hopefully, some of these resources and information are relavant to you too.

The scope of this project is a small piece in the push towards equity and diversity within creative and digital spaces. This project exists currently as a speculative design idea, focusing on the needs of Western Sydney and Blue Mountains creative communities. As such, to foster futher movements of creative resilience and solidarity, we must look to the needs of our own communities and ensure spaces are made for the marginalised voices within it, so that we can create futures that support us all.
